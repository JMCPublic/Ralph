28 April 2026 — Session 1 🚀 Launch day
What we built
Ralph live on the internet at jmcpublic.github.io/Ralph — a real URL, a real page
Mobile-first design with voice search — tap the mic, say a brand name, get a result instantly
Desktop QR code nudge — non-mobile visitors see a full-screen prompt with a QR code pointing to the mobile site
Smart not-found card — searching an unknown brand returns "No TSB link yet — be the first" with a pre-filled mailto button for that exact brand
Search chips — Monzo, Freetrade, Chase, Wise, Octopus Energy, TSB
Mic animation — sonar rings pulse outward and a Listening label appears so it is completely clear the mic is active
Design decisions
Palette Navy & cream — Option C. #185FA5 primary, #042C53 deep, #FDFCF8 background.
Font EB Garamond headings, DM Sans body. Outfit noted for a future redesign pass.
Product thinking
Identified ShareMyLink.co.uk as a comparable — concluded Ralph's differentiator is the fairness narrative and warm entry point, not the mechanic alone
Ruled out WhatsApp Business API — Meta as gatekeeper is a strategic risk and cost is unjustified at this stage
Solution: mobile-first web with voice search replicates the WhatsApp bot experience with no API dependency and no ongoing cost
The not-found card turns a dead end into an acquisition moment — Holly becomes Michael
Ideas parked — loyalty layer
Piggyback messageWhen Michael submits a link Ralph replies asking him to forward a ready-made Ralph introduction to his contacts. One tap, GDPR clean.
Draw mechanicEntries earned per contact who signs up via Michael's message. Free to enter, meaningful reward.
Retroactive rewardWhen Ralph can afford prizes, go back to day-one sharers and include them. Early adopters feel seen.
Charity optionRalph randomly picks 3 smaller lesser-known charities. Michael chooses one. Differentiates Ralph from transactional directories.
