Decisions made
Pre-fill placeholder
The "Brand name here" and "Paste your referral link here" pre-fill text is a guide, not a lock. People can write anywhere in the email — this is a feature, not a bug. No fix needed.
Email client breadth
The mailto link should open whatever the person has set as their default email app. Gmail-only behaviour during testing is likely a device/browser default setting, not a Ralph limitation. Flag to test on multiple devices.
Bugs found in testing
!
Subject line pre-fill not working correctly — shows "Brand name here" as literal text rather than the brand. Needs fixing in the mailto href.
!
Nav "Submit a link" button does nothing — anchor link to #submit section not scrolling. Small fix needed.
!
Post-send UX missing — after the email fires, the person is left in their email app with no way back. Need a thank-you / "got another link?" moment that returns them to the site.

Design decisions made
Colour & typography
Navy deep (#042C53) and cream (#FDFCF8). EB Garamond serif for headings, DM Sans for body. Outfit font noted as a future alternative — cleaner, modern fintech feel.
Branding
Product name: Ralph Shares Links — big RALPH, small "Shares Links" as a strapline. Test inbox: ralph.shares.links@gmail.com.
Hosting
GitHub Pages over Webflow. Own and control the data. Open, exportable, no third-party lock-in.
Onboarding (OBv3)
Person A submits via mailto pre-fill — one tap, no form, no account. Ralph receives, queues. GDPR clean — Ralph never stores contact email addresses.
Opportunity Mechanism (OMv3)
One queue per product, one live link at a time. Opportunity counted on product card click. Budget −1 per visit. Rotation at zero. Solidarity lift and paid promotion ready for later.
Monetisation (MOv3 — agreed so far)
Michael is free. Revenue from brands. Two-tier queue: brand featured slot + Michael's link queue inside brand card. One queue engine, two tiers. Pay-per-click via Ralph's own redirect.
